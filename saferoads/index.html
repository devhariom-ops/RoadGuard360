Traffic Rules Learning Module

Master every traffic rule with our comprehensive interactive guide

Use Zebra Crossings

Always use designated zebra crossings. Never jaywalk even if the road seems empty.

Obey Walk Signals

Wait for the green pedestrian signal before crossing. The red man means STOP.

Look Both Ways

Even at green signal, look left-right-left before crossing. Stay alert always.

No Phone While Walking

Avoid using mobile phones while crossing the road. Distraction kills.

Night Visibility

Wear bright/reflective clothing at night. Walk on the right side facing traffic.

Children's Safety

Hold children's hands firmly near roads. Teach them traffic rules from an early age.

Helmet is Mandatory

ISI-certified helmet for both rider and pillion. Fine ₹1,000 + license suspension without helmet.

Speed Limits

Urban: 50 km/h | Rural: 60 km/h | Highway: 80 km/h for two-wheelers.

Lane Discipline

Ride in the left lane. Avoid weaving between lanes. Use turn signals before every lane change.

No Triple Riding

Only the rider and one pillion allowed. Triple riding is illegal and dangerous.

Use Headlights

Mandatory headlight usage even during daytime for better visibility to other drivers.

No Drunk Riding

Blood Alcohol Limit: 30mg/100ml. Drunk riding leads to fine, jail, and death.

Seatbelt for All

Mandatory for driver and all passengers. Reduces fatality risk by 45%. Fine: ₹1,000.

Safe Overtaking

Overtake from the right side only. Check mirrors, signal, ensure clear vision before overtaking.

Parking Rules

Never park on zebra crossings, bus stops, or fire hydrant zones. Use designated parking areas.

Use Indicators

Indicate 30 meters before turning. Never use hazard lights while driving in rain.

No Phone Driving

Using phone while driving: Fine ₹5,000 + 3-month license suspension.

Child Safety Seat

Children below 4 years must use car seats. Airbag deployment can be fatal for children in front seats.

Lane Discipline

Slow vehicles: left lane | Medium: middle | Fast: right lane. Never straddle lanes.

Emergency Lane

Emergency lane is ONLY for ambulance, fire, and police. Misuse: Fine ₹10,000.

Night Driving

Use high beam only when no oncoming traffic. Take regular breaks every 2 hours.

Heavy Vehicles

Maintain safe following distance of at least 50m behind trucks and heavy vehicles.

Fuel & Tyres

Check tyres, fuel and fluids before long drives. Tyre blowout causes 7% highway deaths.

Rain Driving

Reduce speed by 30% in rain. Keep wipers and headlights on. Double the following distance.

Post-Accident First Aid

1
Assess Safety

Ensure the scene is safe. Turn off engines. Switch on hazard lights. Place warning triangles 50m behind.

2
Call for Help

Call 108 (ambulance) and 112 (police) immediately. Give exact location, number of casualties.

3
Provide First Aid

Do not move injured persons unless in immediate danger. Apply pressure to bleeding wounds. Keep victim warm and calm.

4
Do NOT Do

Don't remove helmets unless unconscious. Don't give food/water. Don't move vehicles before police arrive.

5
CPR if Needed

If victim is unresponsive & not breathing: 30 chest compressions + 2 rescue breaths. Continue till help arrives.

6
Document

Photograph the scene, note vehicle numbers. Collect witness information. File FIR within 24 hours.
